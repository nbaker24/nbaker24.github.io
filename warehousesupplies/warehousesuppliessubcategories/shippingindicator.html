ShockWatch® 2
Bi-directional, vertical response impact indicator detects mishandling of products during transit or storage.
  • Devices turn bright red when an impact occurs.
  • Activate devices by removing adhesive liner.
  • Small, 1 2/3 x 1 2/3" indicator with optional, 3 3/4 x 3 3/4" label for added visibility.
  • Unique serial number for tamper-proof tracking.
  • Companion labels (sold separately) inform handlers of the proper procedures to follow when in receipt of damaged shipments.
  • Alert Tape (sold separately) warns that package is being monitored.

Tip-N-Tell® Indicator
Immediately alert recipients of possible damage to packages during transit.
  • Brightly colored device is easily recognized by package handlers.
  • Simply attach to the outside of your package and pull the bar in the center of the device to activate.
  • Warning labels (included) give clear instructions for the receiver.
  • If the top arrow turns blue, package has been tipped and possible damage has occurred.

Humidity Indicators
Small paper instruments designed to monitor humidity levels.
  • Helps provide visual proof of the relative humidity within a sealed package or given environment.
  • Color changes from blue to pink as humidity levels increase.
  • The 3 dot cards are recommended to monitor electronic equipment or optical instruments and the 6 dot card is for all-purpose use.

Drop-N-Tell®
Self-stick detectors indicate possible shock damage to packages during shipment.
  • Attaches easily to the outside of products or packaging.
  • Warning labels (included) give clear instructions for the receiver.
  • Available in 6 different G-ranges; the higher the G-force, the greater the impact is needed to activate.
  • Non-resettable.

TiltWatch®
Monitor shipments that must remain upright.
  • Foolproof indicator turns white to red if package has been tilted or turned on its side.
  • Remains unaffected by movement due to normal handling conditions.
  • Activates at a 80° tilt and cannot be reset.
  • Companion Labels STWCL (sold separately) inform receivers of proper procedures when mishandling has occurred.
